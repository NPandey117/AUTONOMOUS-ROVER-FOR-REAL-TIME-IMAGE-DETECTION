
# Bill of Materials (BOM)

This table outlines the hardware components used in the system. All components are selected to ensure compliance with the 3 kg weight limit and 25cm X 25cm X 25cm size constraint.

| S.No | Component | Specification | Qty | Description |
| :---: | :--- | :--- | :---: | :--- |
| 1 | **Raspberry Pi 4 Model B** | 8GB RAM | 1 | Main processing unit responsible for running the vision model, camera control, and system logic. |
| 2 | **Raspberry Pi Camera Module** | Module 3/4 Model B | 1 | Captures images and video frames for the object detection system. |
| 3 | **MG995 Servo Motor** | 360 deg continuous rotation | 1 | Rotates the camera platform to perform 180 deg environmental scanning. |
| 4 | **TCRT5000 IR Sensor Array** | 5 Channel | 1 | Detects black line patterns for line-following navigation. |
| 5 | **Motor Driver** | L298N | 1 | Controls the direction and speed of the DC motors using PWM signals. |
| 6 | **DC Motors** | 12V | 2 | Drives the wheels of the robot for movement and navigation. |
| 7 | **Bot body** | Differential Drive Platform | 1 | Structural frame that holds all mechanical and electronic components. |
| 8 | **Buck Converter** | 5V, 3A Output | 1 | Regulates battery voltage to a stable 5V supply for the Raspberry Pi. |
| 9 | **Battery Pack** | 11.1V Li-Po | 1 | Primary power source for the entire robotic system. |
| 10 | **Jumper Wires** | Male-Male / Male-Female | Multiple | Used for electrical connections between components. |
| 11 | **Breadboard** | Prototyping Board | 1 | Used for circuit prototyping and testing connections. |
